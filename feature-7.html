Feature 7

Walking along Route 440

Walking along Route 440

There are several businesses along the Route, with most of them being car dealerships. Interestingly enough, the businesses do seem to fit together, although the type of neighborhood being developed along the West Side neighborhood does not fit at all with it. To explain, it is important to consider that Rt 440 was already established long before the new development of West Side. Hence, the businesses around Rt 440 are tailored to what is typically seen along a ‘highway’ of the magnitude of Rt 440, which are car dealerships. On the other hand, the area surrounding West Side is relatively new and has a different development to it in the sense that there are more urban structures and luxury apartments. However, the reason why each type of business ‘fits’ their respective area and both exist cohesively is because they seem so far apart. Inexplicably, although they are relatively close, they seem to be well divided. As a result, even if both structures are very different, the transition from one to another isn’t awkward because they are well separated, which is in part due to Rt 440 itself.

The six lanes of traffic act as a landmark divisor, which in essence makes the strip along Route 440 feel like a completely separate entity located miles away from the Westside neighborhood. Thus, it can be said that the shopping strip along Rt 440 is disconnected from the Westside neighborhood.

Walking along Route 440 felt like a dangerous experience. The sidewalk along Route 440 is right next to six busy lanes of traffic with cars flying by at high speeds. Overall walking along 440 is not a pleasant experience, there is a sense of alertness that comes with being next to such a loud and busy street that makes it very pedestrian unfriendly.
