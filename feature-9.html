Feature 9

Luxury Apartments

NJCU West Side Residences

When looking back and comparing Route 440 and feature 4 to Society Hill, the difference is obvious. West Side is much newer and has a lot of urban development going on with its multi-story buildings compared to the already established single family homes in Society Hill.

How Does This New Development Complement The Area?

This new development serves the surrounding area very well. The development of new luxurious apartments hopes to attract more residents in the area of bigger funds. At the moment, the scale of the new multi-story apartments does not at all match the single-family homes that surround it and makes the apartments look out of place. However, looking forward to the upcoming years, if these apartments can attract people, then the area will inevitably boom in terms of finance and business.

Unfortunately, this means that people of lower income will be dislodged due to the increase in housing prices. But when taking into consideration the number of opportunities it will bring to Jersey City, the benefits far outweigh its disadvantages

Part of what is driving the development of these new apartments is NJCU. The NJCU dorms and apartment buildings on Westside were “created through a Public/Private Partnership.”. Over the past couple of years, NJCU has strived to expand and make itself a bigger school by building new complexes, and the campus in fort Monmouth. Which in turn has reshaped the surrounding area into one that looks towards growing and making new businesses.

One major conflict faced by the area are shopping districts. As mentioned, the Hudson mall is not the most accessible, and the shopping district along West Side Ave. And Virginia Ave. Is not fit to accommodate all the new development. In texts like …, Jersey City is often depicted as a “cut city”. Meaning that different districts are placed in an erratic fashion that display little city planning thought behind it. Thus, reshaping Jersey City to be better integrated with different sections will be a challenge to solve.

One approach to solving this issue is to integrate these new shopping districts with the new residential development while also reappropriating some of the businesses that exist. For instance, creating small stores along with the buildings. For the latter, convincing bigger companies to invest their business (or partnerships) in Jersey City with the goal of long-term establishment is one way to go about getting bigger and newer brands in the area.
