Feature 5-6

Rt 440 and Hudson Mall

Experencing Route 440 and the Hudson Mall

On the area surrounding the Hudson mall there is very little foot traffic, and the reason become apparently only after trying to access it. The final crossing to Hudson mall is a 6 six lane traffic intersection with many busy cars. Although it may not sound like much, having such a crowded lane gives the area a sense of hostility as one walks along the desolate sidewalks that sit next to such mass of traffic passing by at high speeds. Hence, from an accessibility standpoint the Hudson mall seems to be meant to be accessed by car. Furthermore, to even get to the store one needs to walk across large parking lots. So overall, accessing the Hudson mall on foot is an ordeal in it of itself.

Why Is It Important?

Whether a mall can be accessed by foot might not seem relevant at first glance, but the issue does come into play when a good deal of its customers can be potential university students or residents living in West Campus or coming from NJCU. While it is true that many people in the state have cars, there is also a good number of people not being accounted for that do not own a car. This implies that if students cannot easily access the mall, then many of them will simply only go when it is necessary, which doesn’t not seem like a good business model for a mall.

Even more, when the issue is looked at from a student perspective, as a student I find it cumbersome and exhausting to have to travel to a place that feels so far and hostile. The same issue is encountered by a multitude of residents of whom I’ve asked who find it a chore to go to a nearby store.

This raises greater concern about why there is such a small number of stores in the nearby area or the ones that exist are hard to access. Being that NJCU is a rather medium sized school with a large number of students coming about during class days even if some online classes persist, there is a very small number of stores in the area.

Is it more beneficial that NJCU is in a residential area rather than a commercial one?

NJCU is built upon a residential area and therefore provides some reasoning why there aren’t many stores in place. However, would the overall community of Jersey City benefit more if there was a closer and modern shopping district surrounding NJCU?

The short answer is yes. At the moment, there is a lot of untapped potential in terms of financial growth in the area. NJCU hosts over 5,000 students and faculty, there are multiple schools in the surrounding area that each hold at least 400 students, and lastly John F. Kennedy boulevard is teaming with car traffic. All these factors make a great recipe for a closer shopping district that is modern. Of course, the shopping district of feature 3 was mentioned, but there are many flaws with this district with the first being that is far from campus. Nonetheless, the stores are antiquated and don’t host brands that attract college students.

To summarize, The Jersey City community would benefit greatly if the area around NJCU was built like the college town it was meant to be instead of mediocre housing complexes that bring down the value of NJCU along with it.
