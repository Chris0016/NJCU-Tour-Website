Feature 3

Shopping District

West Side Ave. Shopping District

The walk along West Side Ave from Culver Ave. to Virginia Ave. is analogous to a teenager in puberty. It is an awkward stage where development can be seen towards a new and more mature stage, but traces of its disorderly infancy remain.

There are some areas that show a lot of promise, while others leave a lot to be desired. There are several things to notice when walking along West Side Ave. The first things to notice is that the lack of landscaping from the previous feature transcends into this third feature. This becomes apparent by the lack of any trees.

In addition, something that becomes noticeable are the areas that look inherently dangerous like the one shown below. The image shown below is that on a street that has little lighting, or activity. At night, this street becomes something to avoid.

On the other hand, there are some better features. For instance, the Social Security Administration office that seems to have been deliberately and strategically positioned close to NJCU.

As we head further down into West Side Ave. There is the promise of new areas with luxury development and architecture, but we are quickly reminded of the surrounding area by the odd placement of some luxury apartments next to houses in poor condition. Inevitably, the strange placement of these very different housing schemes makes me wonder about the motives behind the luxury apartments.

Moving along, we make the transition to a shopping district of the area. This shopping district can be best described as cozy and meant to fit a small town. When walking through here the difference in foot traffic becomes notable, since there is a lot more people walking by in a wide range of ages and genders. The area is furthermore decorated by beautiful street art in less noticeable sections, but they provide a better sense of the identity of the area. In other words, patterns of art help to personify the area and give it more culture.

It can be described as a success, since there is a multitude of people walking about and has a mix of home shops and some established store brands as well. Looking into the future, it can be said that this shopping district will see major change in the overall landscape and will ultimately be modernized and adapted for pedestrians.
